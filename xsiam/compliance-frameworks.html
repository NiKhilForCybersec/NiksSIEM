Compliance Implementation Checklist

XSIAM Compliance Readiness

Interview Questions

PCI-DSS Requirement 10 is fundamentally about logging, monitoring, and reviewing access to cardholder data environments. XSIAM is well-positioned to address these requirements through its comprehensive log collection and analysis capabilities.

For 10.2 (audit logging), XSIAM collects logs from all system components through agents, Broker VMs, and cloud integrations. The key is ensuring all CDE systems are configured to send logs - firewalls protecting the CDE, servers processing card data, databases storing card information, and all administrative access points.

For 10.4 (daily log reviews), XSIAM's correlation rules automate the detection of security events that would otherwise require manual review. You configure rules to alert on failed logins, privilege escalations, and access anomalies. The daily workflow becomes reviewing and triaging alerts rather than manually reading logs. Document this process for auditors - show them the rule coverage and the alert handling procedures.

For 10.5 (log retention and protection), XSIAM's retention tiers let you keep logs for the required one year with immediate access to 90 days. The cloud platform provides integrity protection - logs can't be modified once ingested. For audit evidence, you can export specific date ranges and demonstrate the chain of custody.

SOC 2 audits focus on the Trust Services Criteria - security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. XSIAM primarily supports the Security criterion but touches on others as well.

For Security (CC6, CC7), demonstrate continuous monitoring through XSIAM dashboards and alerting. Show auditors your detection coverage map - what threats are you detecting and how quickly? Document your alert handling procedures and provide metrics on mean time to detect and respond. XSIAM's incident timeline feature is excellent for demonstrating your investigation capabilities.

For audit evidence, prepare reports showing: system uptime and availability of the monitoring platform, alert volumes and response times, configuration change tracking, and access reviews for XSIAM itself. Remember that auditors want to see not just that you have tools, but that you're using them effectively.

Document your control environment. Map XSIAM capabilities to specific SOC 2 controls and maintain evidence of control effectiveness. If a control says "security events are reviewed daily," show the audit trail of analysts reviewing and closing alerts daily. XSIAM's reporting features can generate this evidence, but you need to configure the reports in advance of the audit.
