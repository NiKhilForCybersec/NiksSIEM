Attack Surface Management (ASM) with Cortex Xpanse

Cortex Xpanse provides continuous discovery and monitoring of your internet-facing assets, finding exposures before attackers do. This guide covers ASM concepts, Xpanse capabilities, enterprise deployment, and integration with XSIAM for automated remediation.

What is Attack Surface Management?

The Problem ASM Solves

You can't protect what you don't know exists.

Most organizations have significant blind spots in their external attack surface:

  • Shadow IT: Cloud resources spun up outside IT/Security knowledge
  • Forgotten assets: Test servers, old projects, deprecated services
  • M&A blind spots: Inherited infrastructure from acquisitions
  • Third-party exposure: Vendor systems with your data
  • Certificate sprawl: Unknown or expiring SSL certificates

Attackers use the same reconnaissance techniques. ASM lets you find these exposures first.

ASM Definition

Attack Surface Management (ASM) is the continuous process of:

  1. Discovery: Finding all internet-facing assets attributable to your organization
  2. Classification: Categorizing assets by type, ownership, and business criticality
  3. Risk Assessment: Evaluating exposure level and potential impact
  4. Monitoring: Detecting changes, new exposures, and emerging risks
  5. Remediation: Taking action to reduce risk (ideally automated)

Interview Questions

Definition: ASM is the continuous process of discovering, classifying, and monitoring all external-facing assets that could be targeted by attackers.

Why it matters:

  • You can't protect what you don't know exists - Most breaches involve unknown or forgotten assets
  • Attack surface is constantly changing - Cloud resources, M&A, shadow IT
  • Attackers do recon first - ASM lets you find exposures before they do
  • Compliance requirements - PCI-DSS, SOC 2 require asset inventory

Real statistic: "Gartner found that organizations with ASM discovered 30% more assets than they had in their CMDB, and reduced their external attack surface by 25% within 6 months."

Xpanse uses multiple discovery methods:

  1. Internet-wide scanning: Scans all 4.3 billion IPv4 addresses regularly, collecting banners, certificates, and service information
  2. DNS enumeration: Discovers subdomains through zone transfers, certificate transparency, and brute-force
  3. Certificate transparency: Monitors CT logs for new certificates issued to your domains
  4. WHOIS correlation: Attributes IP ranges and domains to your organization
  5. Cloud provider APIs: Optional integration with AWS, Azure, GCP for accurate cloud attribution

Attribution process:

You provide seed data (domain names, IP ranges, company names). Xpanse correlates scan results with this seed data plus public records to attribute assets to your organization with confidence scores.

Immediate response (first 30 minutes):

  1. Confirm the finding - Verify the database is actually exposed and contains sensitive data
  2. Identify the owner - Use Xpanse attribution data, cloud account info, or IP ownership
  3. Block access immediately - Firewall rule, security group change, or take offline if necessary
  4. Preserve evidence - Screenshot of exposure, access logs if available

Investigation (next 24-48 hours):

  1. Check for data access - Review any logs to see if data was accessed during exposure
  2. Determine exposure window - How long was it exposed? When was it created?
  3. Assess data sensitivity - PII, financial, health data? What regulations apply?
  4. Involve legal/compliance - May require breach notification

Remediation:

  1. Implement proper authentication
  2. Move behind firewall/VPN
  3. Enable audit logging
  4. Review similar systems for same issue
  5. Root cause analysis - why was it exposed?

Prioritization framework:

CRITICAL - Fix in 24 hrs Exposed RDP/SSH, unauth databases, admin panels, known exploited CVEs
HIGH - Fix in 72 hrs Exposed APIs, weak SSL, sensitive data exposure, shadow cloud
MEDIUM - Fix in 2 weeks Expiring certs, outdated software, non-sensitive info exposure
LOW - Fix in 30 days Missing security headers, minor misconfigs, best practice gaps

Factors that increase priority:

  • Asset contains sensitive data (PII, financial, health)
  • Asset is business-critical (customer-facing, revenue-generating)
  • Vulnerability has known exploits in the wild
  • Asset is in regulated environment (PCI, HIPAA)
  • Evidence of active scanning or attacks against it
