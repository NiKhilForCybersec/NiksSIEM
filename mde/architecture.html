Interview Questions

A: "ASR (Attack Surface Reduction) rules are built-in Windows protections that block common attack techniques. I'd prioritize enabling: (1) 'Block credential stealing from LSASS' - prevents Mimikatz-style attacks; (2) 'Block Office apps from creating child processes' - stops macro malware; (3) 'Block process creations from PSExec and WMI' - blocks common lateral movement; (4) 'Use advanced ransomware protection' - behavioral ransomware detection. I'd start in Audit mode to identify false positives from legitimate applications, create exclusions, then move to Block mode. The key is phased rollout with monitoring rather than enabling everything at once."

A: "Windows Defender Antivirus is the built-in AV engine that provides real-time protection, signature-based detection, and cloud-delivered protection - it's included in all Windows 10/11. MDE (Defender for Endpoint) adds enterprise EDR capabilities on top: behavioral detection and response, advanced hunting with KQL, threat and vulnerability management, automated investigation and remediation, live response for remote investigation, and centralized management through the security portal. Think of it as: Defender AV = prevention engine, MDE = full EDR platform. MDE requires E5/A5 licensing or standalone purchase, while basic Defender AV is free."
