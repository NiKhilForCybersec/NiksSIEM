Interview Preparation

Describe your approach to developing a new detection use case from scratch.

I follow a structured 6-phase approach:

Phase 1 - Ideation & Prioritization: I start by identifying the threat - either from incident post-mortems, threat intel, MITRE coverage gaps, or compliance requirements. I prioritize based on threat likelihood vs. business impact. A ransomware technique targeting our industry gets higher priority than a theoretical attack we've never seen.

Phase 2 - Research & Design: I research the attack technique thoroughly - MITRE ATT&CK, threat reports, actual malware samples. I identify what artifacts the attacker leaves behind and what data sources would capture them. Then I document the use case: detection hypothesis, data sources needed, expected FP sources, and response actions.

Phase 3 - Development: I write the detection query, starting broad then narrowing. I use accelerated data models where possible for performance. I focus on capturing the behavior, not just IOCs - behaviors are harder for attackers to change.

Phase 4 - Testing: I validate with attack simulation (Atomic Red Team), run against historical data to estimate FP rate, and have analysts review the output. Target is <20% FP rate before deployment.

Phase 5 - Deployment: I deploy in monitor mode first, review daily for the first week, make quick tuning adjustments, then enable full alerting once stable.

Phase 6 - Ongoing: Quarterly reviews to track effectiveness, tune as needed, and retire if no longer relevant.
