Incident Classification

Classify security incidents by type, severity, and impact. Consistent classification enables better metrics, reporting, and response prioritization.

Incident Types

CategoryExamplesTypical Severity
MalwareVirus, ransomware, trojan, wormHigh-Critical
PhishingCredential harvesting, BEC, spear-phishingMedium-High
Unauthorized AccessCompromised account, privilege escalationHigh-Critical
Data BreachExfiltration, exposure, theftCritical
DDoSVolumetric, application layer attacksMedium-High
Insider ThreatData theft, sabotage, policy violationMedium-Critical
Policy ViolationAcceptable use, shadow ITLow-Medium

Severity Levels

Critical

Active breach, widespread impact, regulatory data, business-critical systems

High

Confirmed compromise, significant potential impact, needs immediate attention

Medium

Suspicious activity, limited impact, requires investigation

Low

Minor issues, informational, policy violations

Impact Assessment

Factors to Consider

  • Confidentiality: Was sensitive data exposed or accessed?
  • Integrity: Was data or systems modified?
  • Availability: Are systems or services down?
  • Scope: How many users/systems affected?
  • Regulatory: Is regulated data involved (PII, PCI, HIPAA)?
  • Reputational: Could this damage company reputation?
  • Financial: Direct costs, fines, lost business?

Interview Questions

Severity is based on a combination of factors:

  • Asset criticality: What systems are affected?
  • Data sensitivity: What data is at risk?
  • Scope: How widespread is the impact?
  • Business impact: Is revenue/operations affected?
  • Threat actor capability: Opportunistic vs targeted?

Most organizations have a severity matrix that combines these factors into consistent ratings.
