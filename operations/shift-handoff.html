Shift Handoff

Effective shift handoffs ensure continuity between SOC teams. Learn best practices for documenting ongoing incidents, pending tasks, and situational awareness.

Handoff Components

Active Incidents

Current status, actions taken, next steps, assigned analyst

Pending Tasks

Alerts awaiting review, follow-ups needed, scheduled actions

Situational Awareness

New threats, system changes, maintenance windows, outages

Tool Status

SIEM health, connector issues, known problems

Handoff Best Practices

Effective Handoffs

  • Overlap time: 15-30 minutes for verbal handoff
  • Written + verbal: Document everything, discuss critical items
  • Prioritize: Start with most critical/time-sensitive items
  • Ask questions: Incoming analyst should clarify anything unclear
  • Transfer ownership: Clearly assign open items
  • Update tickets: Ensure all ticket statuses are current

Interview Questions

A good handoff ensures the incoming team has complete situational awareness:

  • Comprehensive: Covers all active work, not just major incidents
  • Prioritized: Most important items first
  • Actionable: Clear next steps for each item
  • Interactive: Opportunity for questions
  • Documented: Written record for reference

Bad handoffs lead to dropped balls, delayed response, and repeated work.
