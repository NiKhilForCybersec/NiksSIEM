Interview Preparation

Walk me through your alert triage process from start to finish.

My triage process follows a structured 5-step approach:

Step 1 - Initial Assessment (< 5 min): Read the alert title, check severity, identify the affected asset and user. I note which detection source triggered it (SIEM rule, EDR, etc.) to understand what we're looking at.

Step 2 - Context Gathering (< 10 min): I enrich the alert with context: asset criticality from CMDB, user role from identity systems, recent alerts on the same asset (last 24-48h), and threat intel on any IOCs. This tells me if this is a high-value target and if there's a pattern.

Step 3 - Benign Pattern Check: Before deep-diving, I check if this matches known benign activity: scheduled maintenance, IT admin tasks, security scanner activity, or previously whitelisted behavior. Many alerts are false positives from legitimate activity.

Step 4 - Threat Validation: If not benign, I validate the threat: check IOCs against threat intel, review process trees or network connections, examine file hashes, and correlate with other alerts. I'm looking for evidence of actual malicious behavior.

Step 5 - Action & Documentation: Based on findings, I either close as FP with tuning recommendations, escalate to T2 with my analysis and specific questions, or if it's a confirmed incident, classify severity and initiate response procedures. Every alert gets documented with findings and time spent.

How do you determine alert severity and when to escalate?

I use a decision tree based on four factors:

1. Active Threat Presence: Is there evidence of hands-on-keyboard activity, active C2, or ransomware execution? → Immediate CRITICAL, escalate now.

2. Data at Risk: Is sensitive data (PII, financial, credentials, IP) potentially exposed or exfiltrated? → HIGH or CRITICAL depending on whether exfil is confirmed.

3. Asset Criticality: Is this a crown jewel system (DC, CA, backup infrastructure, executive laptop)? → Bump severity up one level.

4. Scope of Impact: Single user = lower severity, multiple users or systems = higher, enterprise-wide = CRITICAL.

Escalation triggers: I escalate to T2 when: I need deeper forensic analysis, I suspect lateral movement, the attack technique is beyond my skillset, or I can't determine if it's malicious within 30 minutes. I escalate to T3/IR team for: confirmed active intrusion, any ransomware, data breach evidence, or when containment decisions need senior approval.
