Risk-Based Alerting

Risk-Based Alerting (RBA) aggregates risk from multiple low-fidelity detections to surface high-priority threats while reducing alert fatigue.

RBA Philosophy

The Problem with Traditional Alerting

Traditional approach: Every detection = alert. Result: Alert fatigue. Analysts ignore low-severity alerts, miss real attacks.

RBA approach: Detections add risk scores to entities (users, hosts). Alert when risk threshold exceeded. Low-fidelity signals combine to surface real threats.

How RBA Works

Risk Objects

Entities that accumulate risk: users, hosts, IP addresses

Risk Modifiers

Correlation searches add risk scores to objects

Risk Notables

Alert fires when object exceeds risk threshold

Risk Timeline

Visual history of all risk contributions

Risk Modifier Example

Add Risk Score
// Detection adds 20 risk points to user
| eval risk_object=user
| eval risk_object_type="user"  
| eval risk_score=20
| eval risk_message="User ".user." attempted access to sensitive file"
| collect index=risk

Interview Questions

Traditional: 100 low-severity detections = 100 alerts (ignored)

RBA: 100 detections add risk scores. When user exceeds threshold (e.g., risk > 100), ONE alert fires with full context of all contributing detections.

Result: Fewer, higher-quality alerts. Each alert shows complete picture of suspicious activity.
